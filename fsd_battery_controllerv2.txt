Write a node-red flow which controls the charging and discharging of a battery. This battery is used in a household. The household has solar panels and charges the battery during the day. The goal is to optimize the cost for electricity. 
It is essential to predict the future soc to avoid buying electricity from the grid.
The optimization should prioritize the cost.
Feeding-in too much energy could result in the necessity to charge the battery earlier.

The battery charger can work in different states: 
- "1" means charging from grid. 
- "3" is discharging to compensate the used energy in the household.
- "4" maximum discharge to feed energy back into the grid.

The battery has a capacity of 30kWh. The charger/discharger works with a maximum of 3500W. The average load on the battery is 700W. The soc of the battery is an input parameter and should never fall under 3%. The cost of power changes every 15 minutes.

All relevant data of the household and solar forecast can be found in this influx database (host: 192.168.4.221)

The system should run with the Berlin time.
The price information is updated once a day at 13:00.
The price information is only a part of the total price. For every kwh charged from grid an additional fee of 13ct is added.

A negative price means the grid pays this price on feed-in. 
The battery has a capacity of 30kwh. 
The charge of the battery must be optimized so that it never runs out, has enough free capacity to store the pv power. the battery should can idle, charge , compensate or discharge.

When taking electricity from the grid an additional 13 ct / kwh is added to the price. In the months from 4 to 9 this fee is reduced by 20% in the time between 10:00 to 16:00.

Feed in at a price < 0  means a negative profit when feeding in.
Never feed in at a negative price.
If the price is < -13 charge for profit.
There should be no state 5.
